# Traction

The public repository shows product traction in the form of working surfaces, generated artifacts, and a consistent public narrative.

## Current Signals

- real screenshots from the HELPER UI
- generated desktop application artifacts
- public issue forms for demo and reviewer intake
- public docs and contact surfaces
- a curated showcase repository that is publishable and reviewable

## What This Is Not

- it is not a revenue claim
- it is not a claim of product-market fit
- it is not a claim of completed parity certification

## Why It Still Matters

For early partners or buyers, traction is often a matter of whether the product looks real, repeatable, and structurally coherent. HELPER now has enough surface area to support that conversation honestly.
